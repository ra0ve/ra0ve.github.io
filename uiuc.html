University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign

I earned my Master of Science in Computer Science degree from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. This degree demanded both technical range and academic discipline—especially while balancing a full-time schedule. I completed 32 graduate credits across topics like AI, Embedded Systems, Programming Languages, and Databases, finishing with a 3.8 GPA. My coursework included high-intensity classes like:

  • Systems & Networking – A+
  • Databases – A
  • Artificial Intelligence – B+
  • Data Curation – A+
  • Cloud Computing – A+

The program also required me to complete breadth requirements across four distinct areas and earn at least 12 credits at the graduate level(500) of which I surpassed. UIUC emphasized production-quality understanding of systems, architecture, and algorithms. These weren't abstract theory courses; they were about learning to engineer for scale and resilience.

Unlike undergrad where you’re learning to understand, this program was about learning to build—and defend your choices. It sharpened my thinking around security, systems architecture, and design patterns that hold up under pressure. If MSJC was my foundation and UCR my proving ground, then UIUC was the sharpening stone.
